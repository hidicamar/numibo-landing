1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files a website stores in your browser to remember your actions and preferences. On numibo.com we use them to make the site work, to remember your cookie choices and — only with your consent — for analytics. The application at app.numibo.com additionally uses cookies required for signing in.

2. Essential cookies

These cookies are required for the website and application to function and are always set; without them, signing in and using the service is not possible.

  • Session cookie – keeps you signed in and preserves your settings during a visit; expires after at most 2 hours of inactivity.
  • XSRF-TOKEN – protects forms against abuse (CSRF protection); session cookie.
  • "Remember me" cookie – set only if you choose to stay signed in.
  • cookieyes-consent – remembers the cookie choices you made in the banner; valid for up to 1 year.

3. Analytics cookies (Google Analytics)

On the public website we use Google Analytics to understand site traffic. These cookies are set only with your consent given through the cookie banner. We do not use analytics cookies inside the logged-in area of the application.

  • _ga, _ga_* – distinguishing visitors and measuring traffic; valid for up to 2 years.

We do not use marketing or advertising cookies.

4. How long cookies last

Session cookies are deleted when you close your browser (or after the inactivity period stated above). Persistent cookies — the consent cookie and the analytics cookies — remain until they expire or you delete them.

5. Managing cookies

You can change or withdraw your consent at any time via the cookie settings on the website (the CookieYes banner). You can also delete or block cookies in your browser settings — see the instructions for Chrome, Firefox, Safari and Edge. Blocking essential cookies may prevent the service from working. Our website does not respond to "Do Not Track" browser signals; analytics simply stay off until you consent.

Details on how we process personal data are in our privacy policy; the terms of using the service are in our terms and conditions.
